Right now, the only way to measure ovarian hormones is through doctor visits or expensive at-home tests. But turns out your body can measure ovarian hormones for you.

What if you could measure your hormones at home?

DIY Ovarian Health Tracker

2018 Science Hack Day

Certain cells in your body change shape based on exposure to ovarian hormones

You can measure your exposure to estrogen and progesterone by the daily sampling of these cells.

What if we could make a cheap, reusable bioassay to dynamically measure ovarian hormones?

We developed a microscope set-up to easily image these cells to measure circulating ovarian hormones in our bodies.

With one snapshot and what we know about our cycles, we are able to predict where we are in our menstrual cycle.
